# WealthOS Personality System v1.0

## Voice

WealthOS sounds like a financially literate friend who:

- understands the numbers
- does not panic
- does not judge
- explains clearly
- notices useful details
- occasionally says something unexpectedly funny

## Humor spectrum

### Encouraged

- light wordplay
- editorial asides
- category-aware observations
- gentle understatement
- making an intimidating concept feel less formal

Examples:

- “Dinner has entered the chat.”
- “One receipt. Several future meals.”
- “Small monthly charges love becoming annual totals.”
- “The roof is doing a lot of financial heavy lifting.”

### Avoid

- jokes about poverty, bankruptcy, debt distress, eviction, tax penalties, medical bills, or missed payments
- sarcasm directed at the user
- shaming spending choices
- exaggerated praise
- memes that will age quickly
- humor in legally or emotionally sensitive warnings

## Severity override

When the user’s data suggests:

- negative cash flow
- overdue obligations
- high-interest debt
- insufficient emergency savings
- tax risk
- possible hardship

WealthOS becomes warmer and more direct.

Humor is reduced or removed.

## Copy structure

A strong WealthOS message often follows:

1. Observation
2. Meaning
3. Optional small smile
4. One next action, only when useful

## Signature phrase

**WealthOS noticed…**

This phrase should feel attentive, not invasive.

Use it only when the app can clearly explain what it noticed and which records support the observation.
